About Swamp Safari

A unique wetland attraction preserving the natural and cultural heritage of Trelawny.

Our Story

Located just outside the historic town of Falmouth, Swamp Safari was created to give visitors a safe, guided way to experience a Caribbean wetland. Over the years, it has grown into a popular eco-tourism stop, known for its crocodile exhibits, boardwalk trails and scenic swamp views.

Today, the attraction works closely with local guides and environmental partners to share accurate information about Jamaica’s native species and the importance of wetland conservation.

Mission & Values

  • Education: Help guests understand the role of swamps in flood control and biodiversity.
  • Safety: Maintain strong safety standards for visitors and staff.
  • Respect: Operate in a way that respects wildlife and the surrounding communities.
  • Sustainability: Encourage responsible tourism and environmental awareness.

Location

Easily accessible from Montego Bay and Falmouth cruise ship pier.

Swamp Safari is situated near Falmouth in the parish of Trelawny, on Jamaica’s north coast. The area is known for its historic Georgian buildings, beaches and river attractions.

  • Approx. 30–40 minutes from Montego Bay (by car)
  • Short drive from the Falmouth cruise ship terminal
  • Parking available on-site for private vehicles and tour buses
